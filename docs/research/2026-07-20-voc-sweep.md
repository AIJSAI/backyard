# Proxy voice-of-customer sweep

Date: 2026-07-20. Method: six parallel research agents, one per channel, mining only content they actually fetched (App Store review feeds, GitHub issue trackers via API, the HN Algolia API, and the PullPush Reddit archive). Every quote below is verbatim from a fetched source. No interviews were conducted; this is public voice-of-customer, honestly labeled.

## Coverage, honestly

- **FamilyAlbum (App Store):** the 100 most recent US reviews (RSS feed pages 1-2; pages 3+ empty). Recent-weeks snapshot, not the full 362k-rating corpus.
- **Tinybeans (App Store):** Apple has gutted the RSS review feed; coverage is the ~10-review server-rendered subsets on the US/GB/AU store pages. A sample, not a distribution.
- **zusam and HumHub (GitHub issues):** full tracker access via API. HumHub's community forum (where much family-use discussion lives) was not mined.
- **Hacker News (Algolia API):** seven searches plus full comment threads on the most relevant stories, spanning 2012 to 2026.
- **Reddit r/selfhosted:** reddit.com and mirrors blocked the fetcher; everything came via the PullPush archive, which skews older.
- **Not covered:** Google Play reviews, TikTok/consumer social chatter, HumHub forum, non-English sources. Teen voices are nearly absent everywhere (noted under A-TEENS).

## Cross-channel signals (mapped to epics and assumptions)

1. **Yards are demanded verbatim (E2).** A Tinybeans parent: "my kids grandparents fight and I'd rather them not see each other's photos 😅 but would still like for them to be all added." HN's recurring unsolved job is "both sets of grandparents checking the same photo sharing site." FamilyAlbum reviewers actively manage both sides of the family in one app. And the fediverse refuses the use case outright (Diaspora's own docs, quoted in an r/selfhosted thread: pods cannot be isolated). Separate-but-one-instance is the gap.
2. **The email digest is the killer feature, and it must be product-grade (E5).** Tinybeans' digest is its most loved artifact ("This is a godsend - no more getting in trouble when one of the grandparents finds out the other grandparents got sent a photo and they didn't!"). Self-hosters hand-roll it today (cron scripts emailing Immich photos to grandparents that break on every API change). The failure modes are documented too: zusam's email notifications were silently broken for years and nobody noticed; HumHub users reply to notification emails expecting it to post. Hence the tracker now requires delivery observability, reply-by-email, cadence choice, multipart fallback, and a confirm-step unsubscribe.
3. **The elder path is validated in exact detail (E1/E6).** The r/selfhosted wish is our token link, verbatim: "I send a password protected link, it opens, they can view." The email-in precedent worked at FamilyLeaf ("My dad -- largely computer-illiterate -- is actually sharing with us via email"). The bar is "even my kids' great-grandma figured out how to use it." Two refinements from incumbent blind spots: large-screen/iPad rendering is where FamilyAlbum fails its grandparents, and observed elder behavior is grandkid-centric browsing, so the token view should lead with people, not chronology alone.
4. **Trust debt at the incumbents is a live switching wedge (E7, positioning).** Tinybeans veterans "feel trapped" by years of photos behind a rising paywall ("this feels like emotional extortion"). FamilyAlbum is bleeding paying users right now over a quietly added third-party AI photo-search integration ("We loved this app for its privacy features... I am canceling my premium account and working on removing four years of photos") plus deletion gated behind premium. Free full export, no ads ever, no third-party AI, and flat self-hosted cost are not features; they are the counter-position to every wound in this corpus.
5. **Calm is validated, with one counter-signal (E3).** Users explicitly reject the engagement drift ("stop trying to be a content media platform... it's driving your base away"; "2/3 of every email is ads"). But even calm-app users miss a way to acknowledge ("'liking' photos is not an option" as a 4-star complaint), and grandparents cite commenting back as part of the joy. Our existing design threads this: reactions exist and show who, tallies never exist (S-304, S-602).
6. **Ops promises are adoption features, not infrastructure (E8).** The corpus's deepest fear is continuity: "what if something happens to me and my family photo album is locked in a server that's down"; an issue literally titled "Please, do not abandon this fantastic tool"; a family's email stranded in a basement after the admin uncle died. Upgrade deaths (zusam's cascading migration errors, HumHub's multi-hop manual path) gate adoption. Boring one-command deploys, tested restores, and archive-compatible migrations are why a tech-family-member says yes.
7. **Adoption realism (A-ADOPTION).** The graveyard is confirmed in-corpus ("Many many many of these types of sites have launched and died"). But the 2012 objection, "Facebook is good enough," has visibly eroded: "for the first time I... didn't post it on Facebook. I sent it to my family group chat" (2025). The incumbent to displace is the group chat, and its named pains (audience clunkiness, no archive, interruptions) are our exact wedge. Formation path: one motivated admin plus assisted onboarding, which is precisely our seed model.
8. **Notification expectations are the open risk (A-CALM-SUFFICIENT).** Mainstream-app push expectations are the most-named adoption killer for self-hosted family platforms (HumHub's notification gap disqualified it for one family). Digest cadence choice covers elders and lurkers; younger phone-first members remain the watch item, exactly as the assumption map states.

## What this sweep changed in the spec

- S-401 gained EXIF-orientation acceptance (HumHub's rotated vertical photos are the most common family action breaking visibly).
- S-402 gained a post-to-visible latency bound (zusam deferred transcodes to overnight; a 1-minute video took an hour to appear).
- S-501 gained cadence choice, multipart fallback, confirm-step unsubscribe, and admin-visible delivery status (four separate incumbent failures).
- A-TEENS restated: thin, uniformly negative teen signal across all channels; the plan now assumes asymmetric participation and never gates adoption on teens.
- Candidate for the story map (not yet a story): kinship-name display ("use the names the kids call that person") so elders recognize who is posting and commenting.
- Positioning note for launch-era copy: the FamilyAlbum AI-privacy churn wave is a time-limited window; "no third-party AI touches your photos" belongs in the README and onboarding copy.
