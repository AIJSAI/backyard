# Naming decision record: 32 candidates, 2 waves

Date: 2026-07-19. Method: one research agent per candidate checking GitHub (repos/orgs/users), product collisions via web sweep, domain registration via RDAP/whois, X handle (wave 1 only; dropped as a criterion when the owner chose a single personal account), and language sanity (ES/FR/DE/PT). Verdicts: DEAD = active same-space (family/social/photo) product on the name; CONTESTED = usable but crowded; no candidate came back fully CLEAR.

Wave 1 targeted warm/home metaphors; its finalists were rejected on brand direction (audience median is 15 to 60, not cottagecore) and the brief was re-aimed for wave 2.

## Outcome

**Backyard**, decided 2026-07-19. Full reasoning, accepted risks, and consequences in [ADR-001](../adr/ADR-001-name.md).

Side intel worth keeping: the graveyard of names killed by live products (Hearth Display, Den, Banyan, Kinship x2, Homeroom) doubles as a competitive map. Multiple funded teams are circling family software from the organizer/display angle; none of them ship the async multi-pod feed. The lane Backyard occupies is still empty.
